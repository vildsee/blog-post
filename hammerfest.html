Hammerfest - a hidden gem in the North

Hammerfest is a small 10 000 people town in the island of Kvaløya. The city itself is charming and locals are friendly. If you're into cuisine there are quite a few good restaurants where one can get the taste experienses starting with reindeer burgers and ending with whale sushi! When landscape and hiking is more to your liking you're in for a treat! Hammerfest is built around a bay in the foot of the surrounding mountains which means that from the city center you can take a walk on the old zig-zag road up to the mountain to enjoy some amazing views and northen lights!

In the summer you need to be careful when driving around as there are a lot of reindeer roaming around and they are not in a hurry to get out of the way from a car! I have been stuck behind them quite a few times because they don't have a care in the world walking in the middle of the road! :D

If you have already taken a trip that far north you should definetly also visit Nordkapp aka North Cape which is around 2 hour drive north from Hammerfest. Nordkapp is the northenmost point in Norway located at 70°N. And if you're on your way to south make sure to stop at Alta where they have World Heritage Museum with rock carvings! Around Alta more than 6000 carvings have been found with earliest carving dating back to 4200 BC!
